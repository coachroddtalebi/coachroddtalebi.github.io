Georgia Tech Rowing Team

We are looking for elite high school athletes willing to transition to a new sport and to compete at a high level. True to the name of a student athletes, Georgia Tech rowers distinguish themselves from their peers by prioritizing both their academic and athletic careers.

Rowers are accepted to Georgia Tech because of their academic and professional potential, and only then do they commit themselves to the team and carry on the legacy. Our athletes leave Georgia Tech and stand out amongst their peers, not only with their world class education, but also because of their un-teachable mental strength that allowed them to be fulltime students and fulltime athletes.

The Coaching Staff

In the past two years, Coach Michael Gibel transformed the face of Georgia Tech Men's Rowing. In his short tenure he has brought more Dad Vail's Gold Medals to Tech than the accumulation of all years past. This past spring, he was asked to take over the role to manage the entire program while continuing to coach the men. In addition, Coach Rodd Talebi was asked to take over and coach the women's squad.

For the first time in years, the two squads are working together to make Georgia Tech the premier club for both men's and women's rowing, competing against schools across the country like Grand Valley, Purdue, UGA, and University of California Santa Barbara.

The Deal

This brings us to you. Our hope is to find those individuals ready to take on a new challenge both academically and athletically. Do you have what it takes to study with the worlds brightest? Do you have the mental and physical strength to endure and throw down with the country's most talented? Do you have the guts to welcome the challenges that others would run from? Then we are looking for you.

We can connect you with our athletes in similar fields of study, and potentially give guidance on your application to Georgia Tech. Our network gives you access to students currently enrolled for advice on classes, and to young professionals for building connections. Starting Day 1 of your freshman year, you will have a whole support system behind you, ready to build you up as an athlete, as a student, and as a professional.
